Help

Welcome to the help page for Join, your guide to using our kanban project management tool. Below, we’ll provide an overview of what Join is, how it can benefit you, and how to use it.

What is Join?

Join is a kanban-based project management tool developed by a group of dedicated students as part of their web development bootcamp at the Developer Akademie.

Kanban, meaning "signboard" in Japanese, is a highly efficient approach to visualizing tasks, managing work in progress, and improving overall productivity. Join uses kanban principles to help users organize tasks and projects using a simple and visual interface.

Note that Join is an educational project, not intended for extensive business use. While we aim to provide the best experience, we cannot guarantee full availability, reliability, or accuracy regarding Join’s functionality.

How to Use Join

Follow these steps to start using Join:

1.

Exploring the Board

Once logged in, you'll see a default board. This board represents your project, divided into four sections: "To Do", "In Progress", "Await Feedback", and "Done".

2.

Creating Contacts

In Join, you can add contacts to collaborate on your projects. Navigate to the "Contacts" section, click "New Contact", and enter the required details. Once added, these contacts can be assigned to tasks and interact with the project board.

3.

Adding Cards

After setting up your contacts, you can begin adding cards to represent individual tasks. Click the "+" button under any list to create a new card. You can add task details like name, description, due date, and assign contacts to each task.

4.

Moving Cards

As tasks progress, you can move them across lists by dragging and dropping the cards from one section to another, allowing you to visually track the current status of each task.

5.

Deleting Cards

Once a task is finished, you can either move it to the "Done" list or delete it. Be cautious when deleting tasks, as this action cannot be undone.

To use Join effectively, regular updates from you and your team are necessary to keep the board accurate and up to date.
